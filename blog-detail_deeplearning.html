Deep Learning Checklist for Beginners

Feb 18, 2024by Alpacabar
Embarking on the journey of learning deep learning can be both exciting but very daunting especially for people like me who have just little grasp about it. Without any proper guidance, it made me feel overwhelming. What kind of tools, concepts or techniques to use... Hence after sometimes ponder about it, I figured that I should share my checklist so that you won't be troubled! (i did ㅠㅠㅠㅠㅠ)

I am an J (inf(and a freaking)J. So without structured plan or roadmap, I will spend forever just to start something. This organized checklist may helps to prevent you (too) the feeling of being lost or unsure about what to learn next!

1. Foundational knowledge: Knowing basic of machine learning and neural networks - like Supervised learning, Unsupervised learning and Reinforcement learning might be helpful to start with. Just do not dwell in too much or else you may give up... Also you need to know the basic structure or artificial neural networks too!

2. Programming skills: Of course without programming, it will be nothing. At least you need Python. Familiarize with some popular libraries and frameworks like tensorflow, pytorch, keras.

3. Mathematics Fundamentals: Forgive me if I haven't mentioned beforehand. DL without math is nothing and absolute nothing. I am not strong in math too but you have to be familiar with at least basic calculus, linear algebra and maybe some probability theory

4. Introductory Deep Learning Concepts: After grasped above mentioned basic knowledge, now we are ready to move to some more deep conceptuals like feedforward neural networks, backpropagation, gradient descent. While learning these, it will be more helpful if you are also experimenting with building simple neural network models for basic tasks like classification and regression using deep learning frameworks.

5. Advanced Deep Learning Topics: Now, you are more ready to do the advanced things like Convolutional Neural Networks for Image Processing, Recurrent Neural Networks for sequence data, Generative Adversarial Networks for generating data. You may also want to explore transfer learning, regularisation techniques, optimisation algorithms and hyper parameter tuning to improve model performance.

Of course, different people may have different study styles but for me, having a flow/step, helps me a lot in especially arranging my brain to work best possibly it can. If this helps you, I would be more happier <3 have fun studying!
